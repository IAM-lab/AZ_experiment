Please read the instructions carefully before starting:


You are about to take part in an experiment conducted by the University of Manchester and AstraZeneca.

Why do this study?

We believe better biomarker visualization tools are needed to support scientists and decision makers. Effective knowledge acquisition depends on technologies that support the translation of biomarker data into readily understandable information. This process involves the interaction of access to data, its visualization, and its interpretation. Visualization of data influences: the speed of information interpretation and how that information is interpreted. Please answer honestly and to the best of your knowledge.

What would we be asking you to do?

During this experiment you will be asked to view a series of visualisations (4 in total) based on biomarker data. For each visualization, we would like you to complete a short task. After each task is completed (an answer like “I could not answer” is also valid for completion), we would like to gather your feedback about it.

If you wish to take part in the study you must first read the participant information sheet and provide consent. We will then ask you a few background questions, which include some general questions about reading information from graphs and then the experiment will start.

Please do not use the back or refresh buttons during this study.

Thank you for your time.

What is the purpose of the research?

We are interested in understanding how people use visualisations of biomarker data and other patient-level covariates to inform decisions in clinical trials. These can include clinical, financial, research and resource allocation decisions. This understanding can help to address issues in current usage of visualisations and potentially be used to feedback knowledge into the design of improved future visualisations and improving the presentation of existing visualisations.

Why have I been chosen?

You have been chosen as you represent a user of these types of data visualisations.

What would I be asked to do if I took part?

You will be asked to complete some initial questions about your demographics and professional background. Following this you will complete a short standardized survey that assesses your understanding of reading data from graphs (called the graph literacy scale). You will then be asked to complete 4 short tasks that will involve you using information presented using a visualisation. There are 4 of these tasks in total.

Participants are randomly allocated into one of 3 groups at the start of the experiment. The group will determine if you are shown additional information with the visualisation or not. Following each task you will be asked a series of short questions relating to the task and what you thought about it.

The study can be completed online at a time and place of your own choosing. Progress is not saved so you will need to complete the study in a single sitting. We recommend that you allow up to 45 minutes for this. A subset of participants will be asked to complete this study in person and will additionally have their gaze (eye-movements) recorded with an eye-tracker. This will only be used with people that are visited in person and not those that complete the survey online only.

Will my participation in the study be confidential?

Yes - your participation in the study will be kept confidential to the study team. We do not record your name or other personally identifiable information using the survey. Participants will be allocated a code. For those taking part in the eye-tracking sub-group the code will be used to match their questionnaire data to their eye-tracking data. Names will not be associated with the data at any point. Once we have collected the data, we will not know which individual it belongs to.

What happens to the data collected?

All data will be encrypted and stored securely. Any information that could be used to identify individuals will be anonymised. The data collected may also be passed in anonymous form to other researchers for future analysis.

How is confidentiality maintained?

Participants will be allocated a code, which will be used to match their data with their answers. Names will not be associated with the data at any point.

What happens if I do not want to take part or if I change my mind?

It is up to you to decide whether or not to take part. If you do decide to take part you will be asked to check the box below to indicate that you have read and understood this information and consent to take part. If you decide to take part, you are still free to withdraw at any time without giving a reason and without detriment to yourself. However, it will not be possible to remove your data from the project once it has been anonymised and forms part of the dataset, as we will not be able to identify your specific data. This does not affect your data protection rights.

Will my data be used for future research?

When you agree to take part in a research study, the information (in anonymous form) may be provided to researchers running other research studies in this organisation. The future research should not be incompatible with this research project and will concern the use of visualisations for clinical decision making. These organisations may be universities, NHS organisations in this country or abroad. Your information will only be used by organisations and researchers to conduct research in accordance with the UK Policy Framework for Health and Social Care Research.

This information will not identify you and will not be combined with other information in a way that could identify you. The information will only be used for the purpose of research, and cannot be used to contact you regarding any other matter or to affect your care. It will not be used to make decisions about future services available to you.

Will I be paid for participating in the research?

No, you will not be paid for taking part.

What is the duration of the research?

There is no set time limit for the interview; the length depends on the detail of your answers. Typically this takes around 20-40 minutes.

Where will the research be conducted?

The study will carried out online at a time and place of your convenience unless you are part of the eye-tracking arm. In this instance the study will be carried out at either your place of work in a suitable area (quiet room free of distractions) or at a University of Manchester room or area equipped for carrying out research (i.e. a lab or quiet room).

Will the outcomes of the research be published?

We aim to publish the results of the study, including anonymous quotations in peer reviewed academic publications.

Who has reviewed the research project?

The project has been reviewed by the University of Manchester's University Research Ethics Committee (UREC).
