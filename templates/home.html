Please read the instructions carefully before starting:


You are about to take part in an experiment conducted by the University of Manchester and the Advanced Analytics Centre at AstraZeneca.

We believe better biomarker visualization tools are needed to support scientists and decision makers. Effective knowledge acquisition depends on technologies that support the translation of biomarker data into readily understandable information. This process involves the interaction of access to data, its visualization, and its interpretation. Visualization of data influences: the speed of information interpretation and how that information is interpreted. The results of this study will be shared with you and the remaining participants, we hope you can benefit from them as soon as they become available. Please answer honestly and to the best of your knowledge.

During this experiment you will be asked to view a series of visualisations based on biomarker data. For each visualization, we would like you to complete a short task. After each task is completed (an answer like “I could not answer” is also valid for completion), we would like to gather your feedback about it.

If you wish to take part in the study you must first read the participant information sheet and provide consent. We will then ask you a few background questions, which include some general questions about reading information from graphs and then the experiment will start.
